## What the model loves right now

**Polish banks — three of them.** Alior, Santander Polska and PKO all crack the top 10: high returns on equity, record net interest income, and payout ratios that stay conservative despite 5–7.5% yields. Three banks from one country is also concentration risk — if you rebuild this list, take one, not three.

**Japanese quality with personality.** Sanrio is the company behind Hello Kitty; Toho is the studio behind Godzilla — both with ~7% yields and A- grades, a product of Japan's governance reforms pushing cash to shareholders. The 77 Bank adds the regional-bank angle.

**US specialists, not US standards.** No Coca-Cola, no Johnson & Johnson: right now the model finds the grade-plus-yield combination in GLPI (casino real estate, 6.6%) and Altria (5.6%) — not in the classic aristocrats, whose yields sit below 3%.

## The honest user manual

**High yield + high grade is rare — which is exactly why it's valuable.** Yield alone is often the market pricing a cut. This list filters for both; even so, every position deserves your own check: withholding tax (Poland 19%, Japan 15%), currency risk and foreign-exchange liquidity belong in your math.
