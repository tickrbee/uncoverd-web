Why did **Applied Optoelectronics** drop 17% on a day with *good* news? Because after a ~10x year, a stock doesn't need a reason to fall — it needs a reason to keep rising, every single day. AAOI (NASDAQ: AAOI) was June 9, 2026's most violent reversal.

## The move

AAOI fell **-17.17% to $162.88** (from $196.64) — and the path matters: it *opened higher* at $202.81, touched **$207.60**, then bled all session to a $160.87 low on 26.7M shares. From the intraday peak, that's a **-21.5%** swing. Market cap is still **~$13.1B** — for a stock that traded at **$15.29** within the last twelve months.

## What's driving it

Nothing fundamental — and that's the point:

- **The news flow was positive:** Nvidia **reaffirmed pluggable optics through 2027**, extending demand visibility for AAOI's 800G and 1.6T transceivers; the company has disclosed **$324M+ of hyperscaler orders** and expects **2026 revenue above $1.1B**, with demand exceeding manufacturing capacity through mid-2027.
- **The chart did the selling:** a ~10x in a year, price at **2.3x its own 200-day average** ($71), and a market still twitchy after the Nasdaq's worst session in over a year last Friday. High-momentum AI names de-risk first.
- **The reversal pattern** — gap up, fail, close on the lows — is distribution, not panic: fast money rotating out at the highs.

## The fundamentals

uncoverd rates AAOI **B+**: **Growth 4/5**, **Momentum 4/5** (it was 5/5 days earlier), **Value 3/5**, **Profitability 3/5**, **Health 3/5**. The business case is real — capacity-constrained selling into AI data-center buildouts. The valuation case is the fight: Value 3/5 after the run means the stock price had outrun even an improving story. AAOI pays no dividend; income investors can skip the drama via the [high-yield list](/high-yield).

## Verdict: Sentiment, not story

The drop is **not justified by the news** — if anything, the news got better. It *is* the predictable physics of a parabolic chart: when a stock is up ~10x and trades far above every moving average, a -17% day is the toll, not the verdict. The thesis (AI optics demand > supply through 2027) is intact; the entry price is the only thing that changed — in a buyer's favor. Whether it stabilizes here or closer to its averages is the watch item.
