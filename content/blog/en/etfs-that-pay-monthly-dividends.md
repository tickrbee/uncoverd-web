"Monthly dividend ETF" lists usually copy each other's marketing. We counted actual payments instead: every fund below **verifiably distributed 12 times between June 2025 and May 2026** in our payment database. Here they are, sorted by what the yield really means.

## Tier 1: Ballast (~4%) — bonds that pay like clockwork

| ETF | Verified TTM yield | What it is |
| --- | --- | --- |
| TLT | 4.6% | 20+ year Treasuries |
| BND | 4.0% | Total US bond market |
| AGG | 4.0% | US aggregate bonds |
| SGOV | 3.9% | 0–3 month T-bills |

Bond ETFs pay monthly because coupons arrive monthly. This is the boring tier — and the one that holds when equities don't. SGOV is effectively cash with a yield; TLT is a rate bet wearing a bond costume (it moves hard when yields move).

## Tier 2: Equity income (4–9%) — dividends and defensiveness

**SPHD (4.5%)** holds 50 high-dividend, low-volatility S&P names and pays monthly; **DGRW (1.3%)** is quality dividend *growth*, monthly cadence, modest yield. The standout is **JEPI (8.6%)** — the $30B+ active fund that mixes defensive stocks with an options overlay. It participates in some upside, cushions drawdowns, and has become the default income ETF for a reason.

## Tier 3: Option-premium machines (10–14%) — read this twice

| ETF | Verified TTM yield | Underlying |
| --- | --- | --- |
| QQQI | 14.0% | Nasdaq-100 + calls |
| TLTW | 12.7% | TLT + calls |
| SPYI | 12.1% | S&P 500 + calls |
| QYLD | 11.8% | Nasdaq-100, full overwrite |
| RYLD | 11.7% | Russell 2000, full overwrite |
| XYLD | 10.7% | S&P 500, full overwrite |
| JEPQ | 10.7% | Nasdaq, active overlay |
| PFFA | 9.7% | Preferred stocks, leveraged |
| GPIQ | 9.7% | Nasdaq, active overlay |

These yields are real — we counted the payments — but they're **manufactured from selling call options**, which means three things: upside is capped (a full overwrite like QYLD keeps almost none of a rally), part of the distribution can be **return of your own capital**, and NAV can grind lower across cycles. The newer "high income" generation (SPYI, QQQI) and active overlays (JEPQ, GPIQ) keep more upside than the classic full-overwrite funds — which is why they've eaten QYLD's lunch.
