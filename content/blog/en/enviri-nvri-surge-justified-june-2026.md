Why did **Enviri** jump ~11% to a new high on June 6, 2026? Because the company completed a structural reset — launching as a standalone business and closing a major divestiture — and the market began re-underwriting the "new" entity. NVRI was one of the day's biggest US movers, and the catalyst was corporate rather than operational.

## The move

Enviri (NYSE: NVRI) rallied **+10.99% to $19.70**, above its prior **52-week high (4.72–18.74)**. With **~$1.6B** market cap and **beta of 1.824**, big swings are part of the profile — but a new high still demands a reason, and here there is a concrete one.

## What's driving it

The catalyst is structural. **"New Enviri" launched as a standalone public company**, and the **sale of Clean Earth to Veolia** was completed. This kind of corporate reset can drive sharp repricing: investors re-underwrite the "new" entity, recalibrate peer comparisons, and adjust for leadership and strategy. The release also highlighted leadership under **CEO Russell Hochman**. A cleaner, more focused business is exactly the sort of change that pulls a depressed stock to a new high in a single session.

## The fundamentals

uncoverd rates NVRI **C+ (13/25)** with a lopsided mix: **Momentum 4/5** is strong, but **Value 2/5** and **Health 2/5** are weak. **Growth 2/5** is also soft, while **Profitability 3/5** is average. That combination often describes a stock running on improving sentiment and trend, but without much balance-sheet cushion if expectations get ahead of execution. The strong Momentum score confirms the market is already leaning into the story; the weak Value and Health scores are the reason to stay disciplined about price.

## Verdict: Fair

The standalone launch and Clean Earth sale are a legitimate re-rating event, and NVRI already screens as a momentum name. But with **Value (2/5)** and **Health (2/5)**, the market is giving it credit before the fundamentals (as scored here) look fully supportive — worth respecting, not blindly chasing.
