Ask "which dividend ETF should I buy?" anywhere on the internet and you'll get these three tickers within a minute. They're routinely treated as interchangeable. They aren't — they run three different screens, and when we replayed three real crashes with actual closing prices, **each fund won at least one of them**.

## The basics, verified

| | SCHD | VYM | DGRO |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Trailing yield (verified) | **3.25%** | 2.21% | 1.95% |
| Expense ratio | 0.06% | **0.04%** | 0.08% |
| Payments | quarterly | quarterly | quarterly |
| Screen | ~100 stocks, quality + yield | ~400+ stocks, above-median yield | dividend growers, payout-ratio capped |

Per $10,000 invested, that's roughly **$325, $221 and $195 a year** of income respectively — the spread between SCHD and DGRO is real money, and it buys two different things: current income versus expected payout growth.

## The crash replays

Real closing prices, three real windows, S&P 500 (SPY) as the baseline:

| Window | SPY | SCHD | VYM | DGRO |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| COVID crash 2020 | −34.1% | **−32.7%** | −35.1% | −34.8% |
| Inflation shock 2022 | −25.4% | −16.9% | **−14.6%** | −20.0% |
| Tariff shock 2025 | −19.0% | **−13.7%** | −15.0% | −14.3% |

Three honest observations:

1. **In a liquidity panic, nothing dividend-flavored saves you.** 2020 took everything down ~33–35%; VYM actually fell a point *more* than the index. If your fear is a crash like March 2020, the answer is asset allocation, not ETF selection.
2. **In rate and policy shocks, all three earned their keep.** Beating the index by 5–11 points in 2022 — the year bonds failed too — is exactly what value-tilted dividend portfolios are supposed to do, and they did.
3. **DGRO's growth tilt cuts both ways.** It was the weakest defender of the three in 2022 (−20.0%) because dividend *growers* skew toward the same quality-growth factor the market was punishing. A dividend screen doesn't neutralize a style.

## The verdict

- **Income now → SCHD.** Half again VYM's yield and the best defense in two of three replayed crashes. The concentrated quality screen has been the right kind of picky.
- **One cheap, broad, forget-it fund → VYM.** Lowest fee, 4× the holdings, the best 2022. You give up a third of SCHD's income for maximum breadth.
- **Income a decade from now → DGRO.** Lowest yield today, fastest expected payout growth — and accept that it behaves the most like the growth factor it secretly is.
