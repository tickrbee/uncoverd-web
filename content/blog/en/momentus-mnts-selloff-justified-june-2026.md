Why did **Momentus** fall ~17% on June 8, 2026 — on the same day it announced *more cash*? Because the cash came from selling shares. For a company this small, a $76 million raise is a lot of new stock, and the market priced the dilution the moment the open-high faded.

## The move

Momentus (NASDAQ: MNTS) dropped **-16.8% to $12.41**. The tell is in the intraday path: the stock **opened higher, near $16.38**, then reversed all the way down — a classic "sell-the-raise" reversal. At a market cap of only **~$71–85 million**, MNTS is a micro-cap, and its 52-week range ($3.11 to $43.57) shows just how violently it trades.

## What's driving it

Before the open, Momentus announced **two developments**: it had **strengthened its balance sheet with about $76 million in cash** through capital-raising activity, and it had successfully transitioned its **Vigoride 7 Orbital Service Vehicle** — launched on a SpaceX Transporter mission — into hosted-payload operations.

The mission news is operationally positive. But the dollar figures collide: raising **$76 million** for a company worth only **~$71–85 million** means a large increase in shares outstanding. Existing holders are diluted, and the early pop faded as the market did that math. The backdrop didn't help — space stocks had already been **bleeding on SpaceX pre-IPO fatigue** and the unwind of a high-profile short squeeze.

## The fundamentals

There's a telling gap here: **uncoverd does not rate MNTS.** The composite rating requires enough financial history and data quality to be reliable, and a pre-revenue micro-cap space company doesn't clear that bar. The absence of a grade is itself information — this is a speculative, data-thin name where the investment case rests on milestones and funding, not on profitability or balance-sheet strength.

The raise extends the runway, which is genuinely useful for a cash-hungry business. But "we needed to raise cash near our entire market cap" is not a position of strength.

## The verdict

**Selloff justified.** A capital raise close to the size of the whole company is heavily dilutive, and the reversal from the open is the market correctly repricing the share count, not panic. The Vigoride progress is a real operational positive — but it doesn't offset the dilution in the near term, and the lack of an uncoverd rating underlines how speculative MNTS is.
