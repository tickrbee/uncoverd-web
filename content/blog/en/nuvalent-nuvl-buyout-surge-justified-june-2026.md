Why did **Nuvalent** jump 39% in a session? Because the price stopped being an opinion and became a contract: GSK is buying the company for **$124.00 per share in cash**. Nuvalent (NASDAQ: NUVL) was the market's standout mover on June 9, 2026.

## The move

NUVL rose **+39.28% to $123.25** (from $88.49), a record high, on 51.7M shares — many multiples of normal volume. Market cap landed at **~$9.1B**, just under the deal's **$10.6B** value.

## What's driving it

- **The offer:** GSK will acquire Nuvalent for **$124.00 per share, all cash** — about a **40% premium** and GSK's biggest acquisition in roughly a decade.
- **What GSK gets:** three precision lung-cancer candidates — including **zidesamtinib** (ROS1) and **neladalkib** (ALK) — **two already under FDA review** with decisions expected later this year.
- **The strategic logic:** GSK is rebuilding the oncology franchise it had trimmed, and offsetting HIV-patent expiries; it guides the deal as adding to revenue growth from **2027** and accretive to core EPS by **2029**. GSK's own shares slipped ~3% on the price tag.
- **The fine print:** the usual law-firm "fairness investigations" appeared within hours — standard M&A theater, rarely deal-threatening.

## The fundamentals

uncoverd rated NUVL **B** going in: **Value 3/5, Growth 3/5, Profitability 3/5, Momentum 3/5, Financial Health 4/5** — that Health 4/5 reflects a cash-rich, debt-light clinical-stage balance sheet, exactly the profile an acquirer can underwrite. Nuvalent pays no dividend; if you're here for income, the [screener](/screener) and [high-yield ideas](/high-yield) are the better aisle.

## Verdict: Justified

A cash bid is the cleanest justification a one-day move can have — the price isn't sentiment, it's arithmetic. The stock closed **$0.75 below the offer**, a tight spread that says the market expects the deal to close. But that same math caps the upside: from $123.25, you're collecting cents of arb against months of closing risk. The fundamental trade is over; what remains is merger arbitrage, which is a different job.
