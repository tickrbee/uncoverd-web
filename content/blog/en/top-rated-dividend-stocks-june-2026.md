This is the first edition of a monthly series with one rule: **the model picks, we just verify.** No themes, no narratives — every US-listed dividend payer with a market cap above $5 billion and a yield above 1.5% that currently earns an A-range composite grade. This month that's exactly nine names. We pulled every dividend payment each has made over the past year before publishing, and found something we didn't engineer: **all nine raised their payout within the last twelve months.** Quality and dividend growth turn out to travel together — the model doesn't score dividends directly, yet that's what an A-range business does with its cash.

## What jumps out

**The income end is unusually strong this month.** A 6.5% REIT (GLPI — casino landlords with tenants on long triple-net leases) and Altria at 5.8% both holding A− grades means high yield and high score overlap right now, which is rare; most months the A-list clusters under 3%.

**The raises are the real signal.** East West Bancorp jumping its dividend 33% and Autoliv 24% in a year are boards telling you what they think of their own earnings. A raise is management's forecast with money where its mouth is.

**The honest caveats.** Noble is an offshore driller — the model grades its current value, profitability and balance sheet, all strong, but drilling dividends live and die with contract cycles; treat it as the list's cyclical wildcard. And note who *isn't* here: Coca-Cola (C+), Exxon (C+), Johnson & Johnson (B). The list rewards what a business is worth today, not what its dividend record was.
