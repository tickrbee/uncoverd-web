Most U.S. companies pay dividends quarterly, but a smaller group pays **every month** — handy if you want income that lines up with monthly bills or you simply prefer to reinvest more often.

## Who pays monthly?

Monthly dividends cluster in a few corners of the market:

- **REITs** (real estate investment trusts) such as Realty Income — required to distribute most of their taxable income
- **BDCs** (business development companies) like Main Street Capital
- **Closed-end funds and some covered-call ETFs** built specifically for monthly distributions

## What to look for

A monthly schedule is a convenience, not a quality signal. Screen the same way you would any dividend stock:

| Check | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| **Payout ratio / coverage** | A dividend you can't cover gets cut — frequency won't save it |
| **Yield in context** | Double-digit monthly yields often carry real risk; compare to peers |
| **Distribution history** | Look for steady or rising payments, not a recent spike |
| **Balance sheet** | Leverage is common in REITs/BDCs — check it isn't excessive |
