The market has split in two: AI stocks that pay you nothing, and dividend stocks with no AI story. The overlap — real AI exposure *and* a real dividend — is a short list, and most articles covering it just recite tickers. We ranked the list with our five-pillar model (value, growth, profitability, momentum, balance-sheet health, scored against industry peers) and verified every yield against actual payments in our database.

## The ranking

| Rank | Stock | Grade | Yield (TTM, verified) | AI angle |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | TSMC (TSM) | **B+** | 0.75% | Makes virtually every leading-edge AI chip |
| 2 | Microsoft (MSFT) | **B+** | 0.89% | Azure + Copilot + OpenAI stake |
| 3 | IBM (IBM) | B | **2.44%** | Enterprise AI (watsonx) + consulting |
| 4 | Texas Instruments (TXN) | B | 1.89% | Analog chips in every AI data center's power path |
| 5 | Qualcomm (QCOM) | B | 1.75% | On-device AI in phones and PCs |
| 6 | Analog Devices (ADI) | B | 0.99% | High-performance analog for AI infrastructure |
| 7 | Broadcom (AVGO) | C+ | 0.64% | Custom AI accelerators + networking |

**The quality leaders are the two B+ names.** TSMC is the most structurally important company in the AI buildout — every Nvidia, AMD and custom hyperscaler chip is fabbed there — and it pays a real, growing dividend while compounding earnings. Microsoft is the same thesis one layer up the stack. Neither yields 1%, and neither needs to: these are growth-of-income positions.

**The income pick is IBM.** At ~2.4% it pays roughly three times Microsoft's yield with a credible enterprise-AI franchise. It earns a B, not a B+ — slower growth pillar — but it's the only name here whose yield is meaningful from day one.

**The surprise is Broadcom at C+.** It's arguably the second-biggest AI chip winner after Nvidia, and the market knows it — which is the problem. After the run, the valuation pillar drags the composite to C+ and the yield is down to ~0.64%. Nothing about the business is broken; you're simply paying a price the model can't call attractive. The label "AI dividend stock" is doing a lot of work at that yield.

**The honorable exclusion: Nvidia.** Yes, it pays a dividend — about a penny a quarter, a 0.02% yield. Listing it in an income article (plenty of sites do) is decoration, not analysis.
