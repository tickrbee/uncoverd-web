## The case for: the crash replays are spectacular

When we [replayed real crashes](/blog/recession-resistant-dividend-stocks) with actual closing prices, KO was one of the best defenders we measured: it gave up seven points while the 2022 market lost twenty-five, and barely two while the 2025 tariff shock took nineteen. That behavior — plus a payout raised every year for six decades, through every recession since the 1960s — is what "sleep-well money" looks like in the data, not just the brochure. If your portfolio's problem is fragility, KO is a legitimate prescription.

## The case against: the grade

The model gives KO a **C+**, and it's worth being precise about why, because it's *not* the dividend. Profitability pillars score fine — the margin machine is intact. What drags the composite is **valuation and growth**: you're paying a premium multiple for low-single-digit volume growth, meaning the market has already priced the century of reliability. The C+ is the model saying the same thing two of our other deep-dives found independently: the [Buffett re-rating](/blog/warren-buffett-dividend-stocks-rerated) showed his KO genius was the 1988 entry price (he earns 60%+ a year on cost — you'd start at 2.5%), and the [June A-list](/blog/top-rated-dividend-stocks-june-2026) shows what currently clears the quality-*and*-price bar instead. Greatness fully priced is a C+, and that's exactly how it should work.

## The verdict

**Already own it? Keep it.** Selling a six-decade compounder with measured crash resistance over a valuation quibble is how people end up regretting tidy decisions.

**Buying for income today?** At ~2.5% you need roughly $480,000 of KO for [$1,000 a month](/blog/how-much-for-1000-a-month-in-dividends) — double what a B-or-better 5% blend requires. KO can be a portfolio's spine, but it can't be its engine.

**Waiting?** Then be honest about what you're waiting for: either a price that moves the valuation pillar (KO near a 3.5% yield has historically been the fat pitch) or a model upgrade you can check anytime on its rating page.
