Why did **CECO Environmental** rip 18.5% to an all-time high? Because the market got its first proof-point that the Thermon deal — the company's biggest swing — is landing. CECO (NASDAQ: CECO) was one of June 9, 2026's strongest industrial movers.

## The move

CECO jumped **+18.52% to $95.45** (from $80.53), touching a record **$95.68** intraday. The stock is now up roughly **3.6x from its 52-week low** of $26.32, with the market cap at **~$3.4B** — and the 50-day average ($73.19) far below the tape.

## What's driving it

- **Thermon update:** roughly a week after closing the acquisition of **Thermon Group** — a global leader in industrial process heating — CECO told investors the initial integration is **"on track and delivering immediate benefits"** (CEO Todd Gleason).
- **The M&A call:** management hosted a dedicated call the same day walking through deal economics; the transcript hit the wires and the stock kept climbing.
- **The setup:** CECO had momentum into the news — an environmental/industrial platform compounding through acquisitions, in a market hungry for electrification and industrial-infrastructure exposure.

## The fundamentals

uncoverd rates CECO **B+**, upgraded from B in early June: **Growth 4/5**, **Profitability 4/5**, **Value 3/5**, **Health 3/5**, **Momentum 3/5**. The upgrade preceded the pop — the fundamentals were improving before the headline. The caveat is the price: Value 3/5 on a chart that nearly quadrupled in a year leaves little room for execution slips. CECO pays no dividend, so income hunters should browse [high-yield ideas](/high-yield) instead.

## Verdict: Mostly justified

The move has a real spine: a closed, scaling acquisition with early confirmation, on top of a genuine fundamentals upgrade (G4/P4). But +18.5% in a session *for an update, not new earnings* is momentum amplifying the story. If you own it, the business is executing; if you're arriving now, you're paying a record price for integration promises that still have quarters to prove.
