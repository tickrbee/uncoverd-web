Raising a dividend for **fifty consecutive years** means raising it through Vietnam, two oil shocks, stagflation, 1987, the dot-com bust, the global financial crisis and a pandemic. The companies that did it — the **Dividend Kings** — are the rarest club in income investing. Most articles recite the club roster. We did something different: we **replayed five decades of actual payments** from our database (history back to 1970) and then asked the only question that matters — *which Kings are still worth owning today?*

## The verified crowns

Counting consecutive calendar years of growing split-adjusted payouts in our payment data:

| Company | Measured streak | Current grade | Yield |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) | 55 years | B | 2.2% |
| Emerson Electric (EMR) | 53 years | B | 1.6% |
| Colgate-Palmolive (CL) | 52 years | C+ | 2.4% |

A methodology note we refuse to bury: we measure **cash actually paid per calendar year**. When a company shifts a payment across a year boundary, our measured streak can read shorter than the official declared-rate count — which is why Coca-Cola and Procter & Gamble, official Kings both, show shorter measured runs in our table. We'd rather count money than press releases; their official crowns are not in dispute.

## The crown is not the grade

Here's what the trophy case hides. **Colgate has raised for 52 straight years and currently grades C+** in our model — the streak is alive, but valuation and momentum have cooled. The same split shows across the famous names: Coca-Cola C+, PepsiCo C+, Exxon C+. None are broken; all are priced for a reputation their current fundamentals only partly support. Meanwhile **JNJ and Emerson hold both the crown and a B grade** — culture plus current quality. That combination, not the crown alone, is the buy signal.

## The near-Kings: often the better deal

Just below the 50-year line sits a tier with king-like cultures and **better current grades**: **Consolidated Edison (44 years, B)**, **Dover (41 years, B+** — the best grade in this entire article), **Sherwin-Williams (40y, B)**, **Target (42y, C+)**, **ADP (42y, C+)** and **McDonald's (36y, B)**. Dover will be a King in nine years; you can own the coronation in advance at a B+ rating. Treat the streak as a **screen, not a verdict**: it eliminates dividend cutters with near-certainty, which is genuinely valuable.
