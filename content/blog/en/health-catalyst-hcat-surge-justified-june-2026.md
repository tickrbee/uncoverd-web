Why did **Health Catalyst** jump 39%? A single, concrete deal: it's selling a business for more cash than the whole company was worth that morning. HCAT (NASDAQ: HCAT) was one of the day's biggest movers on June 4, 2026.

## The move

HCAT surged **+39% to ~$1.86** (from $1.33) on **23 million shares** — heavy, real volume for a name this size, not a thin‑quote blip. Market cap before the news was only about **$137M**.

## What's driving it

A clean, company‑specific catalyst:

- Health Catalyst signed a **definitive agreement to divest Vitalware**, its mid‑revenue‑cycle business, **to Med‑Metrix for $147 million** in cash, framing it as "accelerating strategic transformation."
- That **$147M is larger than HCAT's entire pre‑news market cap (~$137M)** — so the deal is a genuine value unlock, not a rounding error, which is exactly why a stock this small repriced so hard.

## The fundamentals

uncoverd rates HCAT a middling **B** — its Value/Growth/Profitability/Health/Momentum pillars all sit near neutral, reflecting a small, historically low‑margin health‑tech business. In that context the **cash matters more than the unit it's giving up**: a $147M inflow materially de‑risks the balance sheet of a ~$137M company. There's no dividend — this is a turnaround/growth story, not an income one. For names that *could* grow into shareholder returns, see [potential dividend payers](/lists/potential-payers).

## Verdict: Fair — but event-driven

The pop **tracks a real, oversized cash event**, so the direction is justified. Just don't mistake it for a re‑rating of the core business: it's a one‑time divestiture on a low‑margin micro‑cap, and the operating story still has to prove out. Worth watching how management redeploys $147M before calling it a turnaround. Screen for sturdier names in the [stock screener](/screener).
