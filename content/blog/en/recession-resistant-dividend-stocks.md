Every dividend site has a "recession-proof stocks" list. Almost none has tested one. We did: we replayed three real crashes with **actual closing prices from our database** — the COVID crash (Feb 19 – Mar 23, 2020), the inflation shock (Jan 3 – Oct 12, 2022) and the tariff shock (Feb 19 – Apr 8, 2025) — for the market's most commonly recommended "defensive" dividend names. Some earned the label. Some absolutely didn't.

## The baseline: what the market did

Everything below is measured against those numbers. "Resistant" means losing meaningfully less — nothing was immune.

The standouts: **General Mills rose fifteen percent during 2022** — cereal is what people buy when they're scared — and **Johnson & Johnson beat the index in every window we could measure**, including holding −5% twice while the market lost 19–25%. McDonald's losing just −0.7% through the 2025 tariff shock is the picture of a business people don't cut in hard times.

## The cautionary tales — "defensive" names that weren't

This is the section other lists won't print, and it's the most useful one:

- **Verizon: −32.0% in 2022** — *worse than the S&P's −25.4%*. A 6%-yielding telecom is a bond in a costume, and 2022 was a bond massacre. The yield didn't protect the price.
- **Realty Income: −45.6% in the COVID crash** — the gold-standard monthly REIT fell eleven points *more* than the market. The dividend kept arriving (that part of the legend is true); the price absolutely did not hold.
- **Costco: −14.5% in the 2025 tariff window** — a great defensive business, but it entered that crisis at a luxury valuation, and valuation is its own risk.
- **Con Edison: −30.4% in COVID, +11.3% in 2025** — the same "boring utility," opposite outcomes. Liquidity panics punish everything; rate and tariff shocks reward regulated cash flows. **The crisis type matters more than the sector label.**

## How to actually use this

Resistance isn't a stock attribute — it's a *stock × crisis-type* attribute. A genuinely resilient income book mixes the staples-and-healthcare names that survive demand shocks (JNJ, KO, PEP, GIS) with the regulated-cash-flow names that survive policy shocks (DUK, ED, SO) — and sizes the bond-costume yields (VZ, REITs) with eyes open about rate risk.
